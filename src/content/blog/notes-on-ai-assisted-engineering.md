AI-assisted engineering becomes most useful when it shortens the distance between intent and action.
That might mean generating a first pass of a component, investigating code paths quickly, or turning
a rough idea into a more concrete implementation plan.

What it does not replace is judgment. Teams still need to decide what should be built, how much risk
is acceptable, and what quality bar a change needs to meet before it reaches users.

The interesting challenge is learning how to use acceleration without becoming careless.
